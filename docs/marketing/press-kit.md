## Description

**Short:** AstroCode is an automation survival game where you write real code and build real circuits to survive on a hostile alien planet.

**Medium:** Stranded alone on Thyra-7, a planet that killed three expeditions before you, you survive by writing Python-like code to program robots, wiring breadboard circuits to power your base, and eventually training neural networks to make your machines think for themselves. Eight biomes, four NPCs, and a 60-day campaign that takes you from fixing a broken oxygen script to engineering a rescue signal that harmonizes with the planet's electromagnetic nervous system.

**Long:**

AstroCode is the first game that takes a player from writing `print("hello")` to designing neural networks inside a single survival narrative — and makes every step feel like a life-or-death decision, because it is. You crash-land on Thyra-7 with a wrecked ship, six hours of oxygen, and a code terminal. Day one, you write a function to keep yourself breathing. Day sixty, your fleet of AI-driven robots runs a self-sustaining colony while you fire a rescue signal through an alien electromagnetic storm.

The game combines three skill domains that have never shared a single title: programming (a full Python-like language with 60+ challenges), electrical engineering (a visual breadboard builder with real components and real failure states), and machine learning (decision trees, regression, and neural networks trained on data your robots collected). Each domain is introduced by a survival problem, not a tutorial screen. You learn Ohm's law because your battery exploded. You learn PID controllers because your greenhouse overheated.

Four NPCs — a circuit-obsessed human survivor, a glitching AI trapped in a military drone, an ancient alien organism that thinks in geological time, and a holographic research mentor from the first lost expedition — guide the player through increasingly sophisticated engineering challenges. The mystery of why every prior expedition failed drives the narrative toward a climax where human technology and alien planetary systems finally work in harmony.

## Key Features

- Real Python-like scripting language with syntax highlighting, debugger, and meaningful error messages
- Visual breadboard circuit builder with resistors, capacitors, microcontrollers, and real failure states
- 8 programmable robot types — from simple gatherers to AI-driven autonomous explorers
- 8 distinct alien biomes, each introducing new engineering concepts through survival pressure
- Machine learning tier: decision trees, regression models, and neural networks trained on in-game data
- 60-day narrative campaign with 4 NPCs and a mystery spanning four failed expeditions
- No combat — threats are environmental: storms, toxic gas, quakes, temperature extremes
- Automation-as-progression: the game gets easier as the player gets smarter
- Exportable code and circuit designs that map to real-world skills

## Developer Bio

Aureonforge is a solo indie studio founded by a systems engineer with a background in field robotics and software development. AstroCode is the studio's debut title — born from the conviction that the best way to learn programming is to need it, not to be lectured about it. Development is self-funded with a lean team of 4-5 contributors.
